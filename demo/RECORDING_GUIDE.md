# Recording the SwipeHire YC demo — step by step

This is a 30-minute job if you're focused. The demo deck does the heavy lifting; you just need to record it cleanly with narration.

---

## What you'll produce

A 60-second MP4 that shows: founder face → product demo → founder face. 1920×1080. Suitable for upload to the YC application video field.

---

## Before you start (5 min)

1. **Read the script** in `SCRIPT.md` twice out loud. Time yourself. Adjust pace until you hit ~60 seconds.
2. **Plug in your mic** (USB or AirPods). Test that audio is clear.
3. **Close everything** except Chrome and your recording software. Mute notifications (Do Not Disturb on macOS, Focus Assist on Windows).
4. **Open the demo deck** in Chrome:
   `file:///C:/SwipeHire/SwipeHire/demo/index.html`
   Press **F** for fullscreen. Press **Space** to pause the auto-advance — you'll control it manually.

---

## Recording (15 min — multiple takes are normal)

### Option A — OBS Studio (recommended; free; full control)

1. Install from https://obsproject.com if you don't have it.
2. New scene → add a **Display Capture** source for your full screen.
3. Add a **Video Capture Device** source for your webcam → resize and pin it bottom-right at ~25% screen height.
4. Add an **Audio Input Capture** for your mic.
5. Settings → Output → Recording → format MP4, quality "indistinguishable" or "high".
6. Hit **Start Recording**, then walk through the script:
   - **0:00–0:13** — Hide the demo deck (cmd+tab or alt+tab). You're on camera, full screen-share is your face. Say the opening lines.
   - **0:13** — Switch to the demo deck (it should be on slide 1). Press → to advance to slide 2 (visa onboarding) as you say "we use real DOL data."
   - **0:24** — Press → to slide 3 (swipe feed) on "match scores are calibrated."
   - **0:31** — Press → to slide 4 (visa intel detail) on "you see exactly which companies."
   - **0:36** — Press → to slide 5 (apply queue) on "where we can auto-apply."
   - **0:41** — Press → to slide 6 (extension overlay) on "via our browser extension."
   - **0:47** — Press → to slide 7 (honesty dashboard) on "we publish our calibration error."
   - **0:52** — Hide the deck, you're on camera again. Deliver the closing.
   - **1:00** — Hit **Stop Recording**.

### Option B — Loom (faster; cloud)

1. Install Loom desktop or use the web app.
2. Choose Cam + Screen mode.
3. Hit Record. Walk the script the same way as above.
4. After recording, Loom auto-saves to the cloud. Download as MP4.

### Option C — QuickTime (Mac only, no webcam overlay)

1. File → New Screen Recording. Record the full demo with voiceover.
2. Separately, File → New Movie Recording for the webcam-only opening + closing.
3. Edit them together in iMovie. More work but free.

## Editing (10 min, optional but recommended)

You can ship the raw take if it's clean, but a small edit polishes it noticeably.

**Free editor:** DaVinci Resolve (overkill but free) or CapCut Desktop (simpler).

What to edit:
1. **Trim dead air** at the start and end.
2. **Cut between camera and screen** sharply — no fade transitions, just hard cuts. Looks more professional.
3. **Audio:** add a very subtle compressor on the voice track (DaVinci Resolve has one built in). Brings up quiet syllables.
4. **No music.** Background music on YC pitches almost always hurts. Spoken word only.
5. **Subtitles:** burn them in. ~30% of YC partners watch on mute the first time. CapCut has auto-subtitles that work well.

Export settings: H.264 MP4, 1080p, 8 Mbps bitrate. File should be ~80–120 MB for 60 seconds.

---

## Uploading to YC

The YC application accepts:
- A direct upload (MP4)
- A YouTube unlisted link
- A Vimeo unlisted link

**Recommendation:** YouTube unlisted. Faster to upload, plays for partners without download, and you can watch view metrics to see if anyone watched it.

---

## Common mistakes to avoid

| Mistake | Why it hurts |
|---|---|
| Reading from a script visibly | Looks unprepared, breaks eye contact |
| Filler words ("um", "like", "y'know") | Wastes your 60-second budget |
| Background noise | Distracts from your message |
| Bad lighting | Makes you look amateur. Sit facing a window. |
| Tiny webcam in corner | Hard to read your face. Make it ~25% screen height. |
| Music behind voice | Implies you're hiding behind production. |
| "We're going to be the X for Y" | YC partners hear this 200 times a day. |
| Talking about funding | Don't. They know. |
| Forgetting to breathe | Watch a take and listen for breath. If you can't hear breath, you're rushing. |

---

## What good looks like

After your take, watch it back and ask:
- Could a YC partner explain SwipeHire to a colleague after watching this once? If no, you went too abstract.
- Does my opening 13 seconds make me memorable? If no, your hook is weak.
- Does the product demo make it look real? If no, the deck is moving too fast or you're describing instead of showing.
- Would I be proud of this video being shown at a YC group office hours? If no, do another take.

Three to five takes is normal. Pick the best one. Don't keep going past five — your delivery degrades with fatigue.

Good luck.
