## Recording notes

### Setup
1. **Open the demo deck** in a fresh Chrome window: `file:///C:/SwipeHire/SwipeHire/demo/index.html`
2. **Press F** for fullscreen
3. **Press Space** to pause auto-advance — you'll control timing manually with arrow keys during recording
4. **Test once before recording.** Walk through each slide with the script open beside you. Confirm timings match.

### Tools
- **Recording:** OBS Studio (free) or Loom — both record screen + webcam picture-in-picture
- **Webcam framing:** picture-in-picture, bottom-right, ~15% screen size, on for opening + closing only
- **Mic:** anything but laptop mic. AirPods will do; a $30 USB mic is better.

### Layout for the recording
- 1080p (1920×1080). YouTube and YC both prefer this.
- Webcam visible during opening (first 13s) and closing (last 8s).
- Hide webcam during the product demo (39s middle) so the screen is fully visible.

### Key beats to nail
- Pause for 1 second after "auto-apply never worked" — this lets the cut breathe.
- The phrase "calibrated probabilities, not vibes" is the most important line. Hit it deliberately.
- "We never click Submit for you" — slow this down. It's the trust differentiator.
- The closing "We are" — say it confidently, with eye contact.

### What to avoid
- Don't say "we believe" or "we hope" anywhere. Use indicative ("we do", "we are", "we've built").
- Don't mention competitors by name more than once. Jobright in the opening — that's it.
- Don't read the script. Memorize the structure; speak naturally.

## Why these specific words?

This script is engineered to hit four YC partner concerns in 60 seconds:

1. **Founder-market fit (0:00–0:13).** "I'm on STEM OPT, I've used 200 of these" — you're the customer. YC partners weight this very heavily.
2. **Differentiation (0:13–0:30).** "Calibrated probabilities, not vibes" — distinct, memorable, contradicts what every other tool does. Plus "real DOL data" = a moat that's hard to copy.
3. **Product is real (0:30–0:52).** Showing the actual flow — onboarding, swipe, visa intel, auto-apply, extension — makes it impossible to dismiss as vaporware.
4. **Market size (0:52–1:00).** "1M international students per year" gives a TAM number that's both real and explicit.

What's deliberately NOT in this script:
- No traction numbers. You don't have any yet. Don't fake them. YC partners will ask in the interview.
- No team slide. It's a solo demo, that's fine for a pre-seed YC application.
- No "ask." YC knows you want to be funded; you don't need to say it.
- No "AI" used as a buzzword. The word "AI" appears zero times in this script. That's deliberate — it's table stakes; saying it weakens the pitch.

## Backup variants

### Shorter (45 seconds, if your delivery runs long):
- Cut the "honesty dashboard" line.
- Tighten the close to "One million international students graduate every year. None of them have a platform built for them. We are."

### If you want to lead with traction later (after you have any):
- Drop the "applied to 200 jobs" line.
- Replace with "We've onboarded X users in our first Y days. Z% have completed an application through us."
- Move the founder-pain line to the closing.

### If YC partner feedback says "too negative on competitors":
- Replace "Half were ghost jobs. The visa filter was wrong. Auto-apply never worked." with "Most of those tools weren't built for international candidates."
- Loses some specificity but reads as more diplomatic.
